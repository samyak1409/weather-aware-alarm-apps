# Alarm Apps — Locked v1 Specification

Two minimal, pitch-black (OLED #000000) alarm apps, one Flutter monorepo, shared core package.
All decisions below were locked with Samyak on 2026-07-11 after research and real-data validation.

**Priority: Arunoday is the daily-driver lifetime app. Nivaat is the occasional-use niche tool. Build/polish order follows that.**

## Arunoday — dawn alarm (सूर्योदय के पहले की रोशनी)

- **Wake anchor: civil dawn** (sun 6° below horizon), computed **on-device** with NOAA (National Oceanic and Atmospheric Administration, a US scientific and regulatory agency) solar math. No API, works offline forever.
- **Offset**: user-configurable ± hours+minutes on top of dawn (e.g. dawn +2h for lazy phases). Applied to wake alarm.
- **Locations**: multiple saved, exactly **one active**; switching recalculates everything (dawn differs across India: Tonk vs BLR ≈ 21 min today; Guwahati vs Dwarka ≈ 1h40m).
- **Fixed bedtime (auto, editable)**: `bedtime = midpoint(earliest yearly dawn, latest yearly dawn) − 8h`, then clamped so dawn-anchored sleep stays within **[7h, 9h]** all year. **Anchored to pure dawn since 2026-07-12** (user decision): the wake offset moves only the wake alarm, never the bedtime. Yearly extremes are **scanned across all 365 days** (earliest ≈ early Jun, latest ≈ mid-Jan — equation of time; never assume solstices).
- **Bedtime is a full ringing alarm** (user decision), auto-computed, editable.
- **Considered & deferred — seasonal-swing capping (2026-07-12, Samyak's "best of both worlds")**: today bedtime is a single fixed clock time all year, so nightly sleep = `8h ± dawnSwing/2` and the summer↔winter gap _equals_ the location's yearly dawn swing (physics; unavoidable with a truly fixed bedtime). At high latitudes that gap is large (America test: 5h45m–10h15m). The `[7,9]` clamp does **not** change the bedtime or this gap — it only flips the `feasible` flag that drives the "closest compromise" note. Samyak's Option 2: keep the fixed bedtime as-is, but on the _extreme_ days only — where a fixed bedtime would give <7½h (early-summer dawn) or >8½h (late-winter dawn) — shift the **wake** off pure dawn just enough to pull that night back to 7½h / 8½h (wake later than dawn in summer, earlier than dawn in winter). Preserves the fixed-bedtime USP for the bulk of the year while taming the extremes. Not built (adds "wake ≠ dawn on some days", needs its own UI signalling); revisit if the raw gap annoys in practice. If built, the `[minSleep,maxSleep]` window would finally _do_ something (define the clamp), so a tighter `[7½,8½]` would then be meaningful. **Phasing (Samyak, 2026-07-13):** ship it first with the looser **[7, 9]** — it only caps the genuinely extreme nights, so wake stays exactly at dawn on far more days (less "why isn't it dawn?" surprise) — and only later tighten toward [7½, 8½] if wanted. Parked for a future version.
- **Bedtime ritual (v1, simplified 2026-07-12 after real-phone testing)**: the bedtime ring screen shows tomorrow's wake time and one action — "NOT SLEEPY +1h" → stop the ring, ring bedtime again in an hour (id 2999). From the shade: the ring notification is the `alarm` plugin's own (Stop button only — a plugin hard limit; a second button would need a plugin fork, decided against 2026-07-12), and tapping it opens the still-ringing full screen where +1h lives. A companion "+1h" notification was tried and removed — it could only appear once the app woke up, i.e. too late. Removed: the +30m variant and the "tomorrow +1h/+2h" one-time wake shift (too much at ring time; `setOneTimeExtra` stays in the controller for a possible later re-entry). Bedtime editing is ±1h buttons matching the wake-offset dialog, with the exact time picker behind a tap on the displayed time. The +1h re-ring is tonight-only by design (never shifts the real bedtime); it shows on home as "AGAIN hh:mm" and Settings lists it with a ✕ to cancel (mistap recovery). No snooze anywhere — the wake alarm doesn't snooze by philosophy.
- **Scheduling — rolling 7-day batch, and why not an OS-native repeat.** The OS only rings alarms registered with it ahead of time (AlarmManager / AlarmKit); a phone won't reliably let the app wake itself each night to arm the next one. Ordinary _fixed-time_ alarm apps ("07:00 daily") therefore lean on the OS's own **repeat** — a repeating `UNCalendarNotificationTrigger`/AlarmKit schedule on iOS, or a repeating / self-rearming AlarmManager alarm on Android (fire → receiver runs briefly → schedules the next) — which recurs forever at a **fixed** clock time with no app involvement. **Arunoday's wake is a different instant every day** (dawn drifts ~1 min/day), so no single fixed-time repeat can express it; each day is a distinct computed time. v1's answer: register the next 7 days of distinct wake (ids 1000–1007) + bedtime (2000–2007) alarms and **re-top the batch on every app open/resume** (`_recomputeAndResync`; it also cancels obsolete ids, never a ringing one). This **self-sustains** in normal use — opening the app tops it up, and on **Android** the wake ring's full-screen intent foregrounds/cold-starts the app → `resync`, so each morning's ring itself re-fills the window. Residual gaps (narrower than "7 days without opening"): **iOS** — dismissing the AlarmKit _system_ ring doesn't resume the Flutter app, so it leans on app-opens + the opportunistic ~30-min BGAppRefresh; and aggressive **OEM task-killers** — a force-stop can drop pending alarms (the industry-wide "don't kill my app" problem, only ever mitigated by all alarm apps, never fully solved). **v2 fix — background self-rearm:** on fire, a background isolate (Android `android_alarm_manager_plus`, already powering Nivaat's wind checks) recomputes and appends the next day, so the window never drains even if the app is never opened. The 7-day depth is a tunable buffer, not a hard limit.
- Note: dawn is computed from saved lat/lon — pure math, no data stored, no network. Adding a place you're standing at uses a one-shot **GPS** fix (satellite, works offline — shipped in v1); internet is needed only to _search_ for a place you're not at. New city = add/switch location.
- Reference numbers (validation): Jaipur 2026 sunrise 05:32 (10 Jun) → 07:17 (12 Jan), swing 105 min. Tonk civil dawn 05:08→06:51. BLR civil dawn 05:29→06:24 (swing only 55 min). Midpoint−8h for Tonk wake≈dawn: bedtime ≈ 22:25, sleep 7.1–8.9h, avg ≈ 8.0h.

### Design rationale: why civil dawn + fixed bedtime (settled 2026-07-12)

The twilight ladder — morning dark→light: **astronomical dawn** (sun 18° below, first light / pitch-black ends) → nautical (12°) → **civil dawn** (6°, enough light to move outdoors) → sunrise (0°). Evening light→dark mirrors it: sunset → civil dusk (6°) → nautical (12°) → **astronomical dusk** (18°, fully pitch dark, true night begins).

- **Wake anchor = civil dawn (6°)**, not astronomical dawn or sunrise. Considered anchoring wake to _first light_ (astronomical dawn) and bedtime to _last light gone_ (astronomical dusk) — the "true-night" pair, which confines sleep to genuine darkness (~7–8h at the equator, never a 12h sunset→sunrise night). Rejected: (a) "dawn" and "sunrise" are the terms people actually know; (b) true-dark boundaries make the seasonal swing _worse_ and vanish entirely at high latitude in polar summer (undefined). Civil dawn is the pragmatic, universally-defined "usable morning light."
- **Bedtime = fixed clock time (auto = midpoint(yearly dawns) − 8h), not dusk-anchored.** The asymmetry is deliberate: morning light is the dominant circadian _zeitgeber_ (anchoring wake to it is physiologically meaningful), whereas modern sleep-onset is a _habit_, not triggered by darkness (artificial light broke that). A regular bedtime is the strongest controllable lever for sleep regularity; a dusk-anchored bedtime would swing wildly (dark ≈ 18:00 in winter → ~12h in bed) and a dawn-anchored one would wander daily. Fix it instead.
- **Consequence — the summer↔winter sleep gap = the location's dawn swing** (min at equator ≈ 30 min; grows with latitude; huge past ~60°, undefined past the polar circle). This is physics, not a tunable; see the deferred "seasonal-swing capping" note above for the only way to tame it without abandoning the fixed bedtime.
- Honest caveat on the science: these are well-supported _general_ sleep-hygiene principles (regular schedule, morning-light exposure), not a claim that this specific dawn-anchored formula is clinically optimal for any individual. It's a principled, defensible default — not a medical prescription.
- **Science check (researched 2026-07-12):** (1) _Consistency vs. light_ — both matter and are complementary; a consistent wake time anchored by morning light is the strong lever, and a camping/children study found waking near sunrise yields melatonin timing matched to natural sunset. So dawn-anchored wake isn't anti-science — dawn drifts ~1 min/day, so it's effectively a consistent time that also happens to align with light. The mild tension (perfect regularity would freeze the clock) is real but small. (2) _Seasonal sleep_ — humans do sleep modestly more in winter (studies: ~15–20 min longer/night, ~30 min more REM vs spring), and seasonal variation shrinks toward the equator — which is _directionally_ what Arunoday produces (longer winter nights via later dawn). So the summer↔winter swing isn't unhealthy; it loosely tracks a real seasonal need, though the app's swing magnitude is a side-effect of geometry, not a tuned dose.

### UX decisions (Arunoday home/settings, 2026-07-12)

- **Manual bedtime is stored as an offset from auto** (`bedtimeOffsetMinutes`, signed), mirroring wake = offset-from-dawn — so it stays consistent across location switches. Home/settings show "Auto" or "Auto ±H:MM"; the old "Manual" label is gone; picking exactly auto snaps back to Auto. (No data migration — pre-change stored absolutes are disposable test data.)
- **Collision guards (clean, minute-precision):** bedtime can't be set to the same minute as the wake alarm or a pending AGAIN, and wake can't be set to the same minute as the bedtime — a SnackBar explains and the change is refused.
- **Polar / no-daily-dawn locations refused:** adding or selecting a location whose civil dawn is undefined on any day of the year is blocked with "No daily dawn here (polar) — Arunoday needs a real dawn" (`Solar.hasDailyDawnAllYear`). The app's premise requires a real daily dawn.
- **Home label order.** WAKE: `WAKE · DAWN ±H:MM · [IN Xh Ym | OFF]`. BEDTIME: `BEDTIME · [AUTO | AUTO ±H:MM] · [AGAIN hh:mm] · [Xh Ym TONIGHT] · [IN Xh Ym | OFF]`. IN (enabled) and OFF (disabled) share the final slot (opposites). **IN = the next actual ring** = min(daily bedtime, AGAIN); **TONIGHT = max(daily bedtime, AGAIN) → next wake** (when you'll truly turn in). The dead "ONE-TIME" wake label was removed (its setter was retired with the +1h/+2h ritual).
- **Settings is a full-screen page** (2026-07-13), not a bottom sheet — it was already effectively full-height with several locations, and a page means validation SnackBars appear _on it_ (a sheet let them fire on the hidden home Scaffold). The location list fills the page and scrolls with an always-visible **Scrollbar** (users must see there's more). Deleting the **last** location pops the page (settings is unreachable with zero locations — reopen only via "Add location" on home).
- **Polar re-validation on load** (2026-07-13, defensive): `_recomputeAndResync` re-checks `hasDailyDawnAllYear` for the active location every load/resync; if it fails, the plan is nulled and home shows a "No daily dawn here" screen instead of a degenerate plan. (This defends against a real pole saved by an older build — it was NOT the cause of any observed bug; "South Pole" in testing geocoded to _South Pole, Illinois_, a normal ~38°N town.) The strict "dawn every day" rule also blocks high-latitude _cities_ with white-night summers — Anchorage (28 dawn-less days), Reykjavik (66), Tromsø (108). **Kept strict** (Samyak confirmed) — the app's premise is a real daily dawn.
- **Wake-offset dialog dawn bug — FIXED 2026-07-13.** The dialog showed dawn/wake one minute off (e.g. 15:23 while the real dawn/wake was 15:22, provable because setting bedtime to 15:22 collided but 15:23 didn't). Cause: `_anchorDawn` recomputed `dawnOn(nextWake)`, and nextWake's calendar day can differ from the day whose dawn _produced_ it, so the recompute read a neighbour day's (drifted) dawn. Fix: anchor = `nextWake − wakeOffset` exactly. (The device-timezone quirk — a foreign location's dawn shown in device-local time, e.g. Illinois dawn at ~15:22 IST — is separate and expected; it is NOT this bug. Conflating the two last turn was a miss.)
- **Bedtime ±12h clamp** matches the wake offset — and, crucially, the **bedtime dialog now holds the signed offset directly** (like wake), not an absolute clock time. Earlier it stored absolute minutes and folded the offset at the antipode (−12h ≡ +12h), which made a symmetric hard-stop impossible: pressing −1h looped around the clock forever and −12h saved/displayed as +12h. With the signed-offset representation, ±1h hard-stops at **both** edges, −12h stays −12h, and the ±1h **buttons disable at whichever edge is reached**. Shared pure helpers `bumpOffset`/`offsetAtLimit`/`signedBedtimeOffset` (`signedBedtimeOffset` folds only in the one-shot time-picker path, where the sign is genuinely ambiguous). Regression-tested (both `+1h@+12h` stays and `−1h` loop → hard-stops at −12h). Wake dialog also shows **dawn-first** now (`dawn 05:36 · wake 06:30`) — anchor→result, matching "offset from dawn". NB: +12h and −12h are the same clock time (12h from the anchor); wake keeps them as distinct offset _labels_ (both reachable), bedtime's signed offset likewise.
- **Nivaat accent** changed from teal `#6FD6C8` to clear-sky blue `#6FB7EC` (also the AlarmKit tint in engine.dart).
- **Location scrollbar**: hidden by default, fades in while scrolling; dull grey thumb (not bright white) via `scrollbarTheme`; **flashes visible for ~1s when the settings page opens** — but only when the list actually overflows — as a "there's more below" cue.
- **Duplicate-location guard** (2026-07-13, **both apps**): adding a place (search _or_ GPS) that duplicates an existing one is refused.
  - **Arunoday dedups by the actual alarm, not distance** (`existingLocationSameDawn`): distance is the wrong proxy — dawn only shifts ~1 min per ~25 km of longitude (4 min/°), so two spots 15 km apart give the identical alarm. Instead it compares civil dawn **to the minute at 4 sample dates** (both solstices — ~Jun 21 & Dec 21, the year's daylight extremes, where latitudes diverge most — and both equinoxes, ~Mar 21 & Sep 21, when day ≈ night everywhere); matching on all four ⇒ same alarm year-round ⇒ duplicate. Message: "Same dawn as X — already added." (Chose this over a fixed ~20 km radius because the radius is directionally approximate — E–W ≈ 25 km/min but N–S diverges seasonally — whereas the dawn check is exact.)
  - **Nivaat dedups by 100 m** (`existingCourtNear`, true great-circle distance) — wind is spot-specific and distinct courts sit close, so only the exact same spot is rejected.
  - **Validation runs inside the picker, before naming/returning** (2026-07-13): `showLocationSearch(validate:)` takes a `(lat,lon) → String?` callback; on the GPS path it fires the moment the fix lands (so a duplicate/polar spot is rejected _before_ the "name this place" prompt — no wasted effort), and on the search path before the result is returned. The message shows inline in the picker sheet, which stays open so the user can pick another.
- **"Closest compromise" message removed** (2026-07-13): the old "No fixed bedtime fits 7–9h here; showing closest compromise" was jargon that named a problem with no in-app fix. Replaced with a neutral, self-explanatory line: _"Year here: sleep Xh (summer) to Yh (winter) — the natural swing of daylight at this latitude."_ The factual range is the honest signal; the `feasible` flag stays in `SleepPlan` (concept + test) but is no longer surfaced. (If seasonal-swing capping is ever built, a note-with-solution can return.)
- **Offset display has no space before the sign** — `DAWN+0:00`, `AUTO+6:40` (not `DAWN +0:00`). Reads as the literal arithmetic the app performs (wake = dawn **+** offset; bedtime = auto **+** offset) and keeps each label+value a single visual token distinct from the `·` separators.
- **SnackBar themed** to the OLED palette (dark surface, light text) app-wide via `snackBarTheme`, floating — the default was a light box. **GPS copy**: "Getting your location…" / "Couldn't get your location — try search instead" (was "Getting a GPS fix…").
- **Science sources (supportive bias, business-facing):** consistency+light — [Embody](https://liveembody.com/blog/consistent-wake-up-time), [camping/entrainment PMC](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pmc/articles/PMC9756595/); winter sleep — [Frontiers](https://www.frontiersin.org/news/2023/02/17/humans-dont-hibernate-but-we-still-need-more-winter-sleep), [Healthline](https://www.healthline.com/health-news/seasonal-sleeping-why-we-need-more-rest-in-the-winter). Pitched compellingly in README's "Why waking with the dawn works". Keep the "general sleep-hygiene, not medical advice" line.

## Nivaat — wind-conditional badminton alarm (निवात: Gita 6.19, the lamp in a windless place)

- Alarms at **any time of day** (morning or evening play). Each alarm picks a **court** from saved courts.
- **Wind data**: Open-Meteo (free, no key). Hourly forecast for alarm hour when far out; current wind when ≤15 min away.
- **Threshold**: dropdown **4–6 km/h, default 6** — semantic = **court-level wind**. The API reports 10 m wind; app converts: `court_wind = api_wind × 0.6` (log wind profile — **basis & sources in _Wind model_ below**). Validated against real data: raw thresholds would never ring in BLR (July 6am median 16.4 km/h raw). Speed & gust and their caps are compared and shown in **whole km/h** (2026-07-13).
- **Gust rule (auto, uneditable)**: `raw_gust_limit = 2.2 × raw_speed_limit` (no floor). The `2.2×` sits above normal gust factors (~1.4–1.7), so only abnormal gusts block a ring — **basis in _Wind model_ below**. Ring only if BOTH speed and gust pass.
- **Check cascade (revised 2026-07-15)**: **T−1h, −30m, −15m, −10m, −5m, −2m, −1m, T−0**, then every minute to **+30 min**. The far pre-arm rungs (T−12h…−2h) were dropped: the _scheduled_ ladder only ever runs on **Android** (exact AlarmManager wakeups — a wake at T can itself check-and-ring), where the near ladder + T−0 carry it; on **iOS** nothing runs at an exact time, so the ladder is opportunistic (BGAppRefresh) regardless. Far checks use the forecast, within 15 min uses current wind; latest successful check wins. **The +30m retries now run after *any* skip — windy/gusty **and** no-data, not just no-data — so a morning that calms within 30 min rings *late*.** A skip is **provisional** until the cap: at T we post a **"still checking until 6:30" heads-up** (the reason so far + the deadline), keep re-checking, and — if it calms — **ring late**, leaving the heads-up in place (the ring itself is the update); otherwise at +30m we post a **separate** final skip card _alongside_ the heads-up — a distinct notification id, so it alerts afresh rather than quietly overwriting. The final card uses the **last known** reason (persisted in the check state), so a network blip _at_ the cap still says "windy", not "couldn't check". **A ring is final — never retried.**
- **Ring survives app-resume** (2026-07-13, parity with Arunoday): resuming Nivaat runs `resync → evaluateAll`, and once an occurrence has rung its check-state is cleared so re-evaluation resolves the _next_ (future) occurrence. The engine's skip branch used to `cancel(alarm.id)` unconditionally — silencing the live ring by shared id when the next occurrence's forecast was windy. Now it only cancels a ring when evaluating the _current_ occurrence (`next − now ≤ liveWindWindow`), never a future one. Regression-tested (ringing + windy future occurrence ⇒ ring survives).
- **Courts sheet scrolls** (2026-07-13): the Nivaat courts list was a plain `Column` (overflowed with many courts — missed in the first parity pass). Now bounded (≤45% screen height) + scrollable, `isScrollControlled`, with the same dull fade-in scrollbar and 1-second flash-on-open as Arunoday's location list.
- **Deleting a court deletes its alarms** (2026-07-13): previously `removeCourt` only cancelled the rings but kept the alarm objects pointing at a now-dead court id — opening such an alarm assert-crashed the court `DropdownButton` ("exactly one item with value X"). Now the orphaned alarms are removed (rings + checks + cascade state cancelled), behind a confirmation shown when the court has any alarms _or_ history. **The court's history log is deleted alongside its alarms (2026-07-16, user decision — a dead court leaves no orphan log), and the confirmation names both counts** ("2 alarms use {court} and will be deleted too, along with 5 history entries. Continue?"). Every `HistoryRecord` carries its **`courtId`** (added 2026-07-16), so a court delete removes its _entire_ log — including rows from alarms deleted individually earlier; no orphaned history survives. The alarm editor also defensively falls back to the first court if its saved court is missing. Regression-tested.
- **iOS is AlarmKit-only now (2026-07-16, user decision)**: deployment target raised 15 → **26.0** (both apps), and Arunoday's bedtime ring switched from the `alarm` package to AlarmKit — so **every** iOS ring is a real system alarm that survives force-quit and reboot. Why: the `alarm` package's iOS ring is an in-process `Timer` kept alive by a silent audio session (verified in its v5.5.0 Swift source), so a force-quit or reboot kills it and its `trigger: nil` notification is no backup — unacceptable for an alarm. Cost, accepted: the bedtime **"+1h" ritual is Android-only** (AlarmKit alerts are Stop-only; you can still open the app to adjust). `RoutingScheduler` (the old wake-vs-bedtime split) was deleted — one scheduler now serves both.
- **iOS hard-requires AlarmKit — no `alarm`-package fallback (2026-07-18, user decision)**: on iOS `createAlarmScheduler` always returns `AlarmKitScheduler`; there is no drop to the `alarm` package. If the user **denies** AlarmKit, `scheduleRing` catches the `PlatformException` and no-ops (never crashes the engine), and `AlarmPermissionBanner` — a shared core widget on both home screens, driven by `alarmSchedulingDenied()` and re-checked on app-resume — tells the user "alarms are turned off" and opens Settings (`url_launcher` → `app-settings:`). Rationale: the `alarm` package's iOS ring is unreliable enough (Timer + silent-audio) that shipping it as a "fallback" would just hide a broken alarm; better to fail loudly and route the user to the fix. Consequence: `AlarmPkgScheduler` is now **Android-only**, so its `warningNotificationOnKill` is back to `false` (Android genuinely survives kill).
- **Late-first-open finalisation + real ring time (2026-07-18)**: two cascade gaps closed. (1) If Nivaat first runs only **past the +30m cap** for an occurrence (common on iOS), the occurrence is now finalised from the last-known `CheckState` instead of silently rolling to tomorrow — a committed ring logs "rang", otherwise it logs the skip **and posts its one card** (no heads-up, since the window is over). (2) `HistoryRecord` gained **`checkedAt`** (nullable) = when the wind check _behind_ the outcome ran — its freshness, not the ring time. History rows and the skip card **always** show it as reinforcement that the result came from a real check: "· checked HH:MM" for a ring/windy/gusty (from `CheckState.lastCheckAt`, the last successful reading — e.g. an alarm set at 22:00 whose only check ran then shows "checked 22:00" on its 06:00 ring), and "· last tried HH:MM" for a no-data skip (from new `CheckState.lastAttemptAt`, since there was no successful reading). Ring time itself isn't surfaced — with robust alarm APIs the ring fires at its scheduled minute, so what matters is _which check_ it acted on. (Setting an alarm >1h out already checks the forecast immediately — the creation-time `evaluateAlarm` isn't gated by the ladder — so no change was needed there.)
- **iOS BGProcessing + per-rung `earliestBeginDate` (Nivaat, 2026-07-18)**: added a second opportunistic iOS trigger. Alongside the usage-driven periodic **BGAppRefresh** (~30 min), `IosCheckScheduler.scheduleCheck` now registers a **BGProcessingTask** (`com.samyak.nivaat.processing`; declared in Info.plist, handler registered in AppDelegate) each cascade step with `initialDelay = nextRung − now` → its **`earliestBeginDate` tracks the next rung**, so a granted (opportunistic) wakeup lands near T rather than being burned early; re-registered every `evaluateAll` to walk the ladder. `requiresExternalPower: false` (`Constraints.requiresCharging: false` — the check is one tiny HTTP call, so no reason to require the cable → eligible charging-or-not) + network required. Caveats: `earliestBeginDate` is a **floor, not a schedule** (iOS still decides if/when); one shared task, so with multiple alarms the last-scheduled rung wins (the periodic refresh backstops); the re-chain re-submits from the background dispatcher isolate — **builds on iOS but the background _runtime_ is device-only, unverified here** (BGTask failures are silent).
- **Android battery-optimization exemption (Nivaat, 2026-07-18)**: off charger, Doze rate-limits the cascade's `allowWhileIdle` exact alarms to ~once per 9 min and throttles their network, so tight near-T rungs collapse (charging avoids Doze entirely → full cascade). Nivaat now asks **once** at first launch for a battery-optimization exemption via a `nivaat/battery` MethodChannel firing `ACTION_REQUEST_IGNORE_BATTERY_OPTIMIZATIONS` (`requestBatteryExemptionOnce`, SharedPreferences-guarded so it never nags). Same reliability move dedicated alarm apps make. **iOS "Open Settings" moved off `app_settings` (broke on AGP 9) to `url_launcher` (`app-settings:`).**
- **Fail-safe (user decision)**: windy → no ring; API/network fail → no ring. Every skip — windy, gusty, AND no-data — leaves a **normal audible notification + in-app history entry**, and every outcome shows **all four numbers** ("too gusty · wind 3 (≤4) · gusts 16 (≤15) km/h" / "could not check the wind — alarm skipped"). Implemented in v1 (flutter*local_notifications; was silent, made audible 2026-07-12 — channel id bumped to `nivaat_skips_v2` because Android freezes a channel's importance at creation). Trust through honesty, never silent failure. **(2026-07-15: a skip first posts a "still checking until +30m" heads-up while the retry window runs, then either rings late — leaving that card in place — or posts a separate final skip card alongside it; see the check cascade above.)** **iOS notification permission is requested explicitly (2026-07-13)** — the `alarm` package only \_checks* iOS authorization, never requests it, so without this the skip cards (and Arunoday's bedtime banner) would never appear on iOS while working on Android. Both notifiers now request on both platforms.
- **Wind-proportional volume**: linear ramp, **100% at 0 wind → 75% floor at threshold** (raised from 50% on 2026-07-15 — a 4-limit court at speed 2 was ringing at only 75%; the tighter 75–100% band keeps it loud); above threshold → skip. Android: volume computed at ring time (applied via the `alarm` package's enforced `VolumeSettings`). iOS: pre-rendered loudness variants chosen at scheduling (AlarmKit can't set volume).
- iOS reliability: evening decision + BG-refresh ladder (grants are opportunistic); **Bedside mode** (foreground OLED clock, true ring-time check) is the reliable option — v2.
- Expected ring rates (validated simulation, threshold 4→6): Tonk Jan 71→90%, Tonk Jul 25→56%, BLR Jan 23→47%, BLR Jul ~0–6% (monsoon: correctly windy). **These are a ring-rate _sanity check_, not the source of the constants** — the constants come from the general physics/standards below, so the model isn't "calibrated to India and generalised".

### Wind model — scientific basis & honest limits (2026-07-13)

The constants (`×0.6`, the `4–6` range, `gust = 2.2 × speed`) are grounded in location-independent physics and a global sport standard — no region-tuned magic numbers.

- **10 m → court, `×0.6` (log wind profile).** Weather models report wind at the standard **10 m** height; you play at ~2 m, where ground friction slows it. The wind-profile power law gives `wind(2m)/wind(10m) = (2/10)^α = 0.2^α`, α rising with surface roughness ([Wind profile power law, Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wind_profile_power_law): α≈0.143 open land, 0.11 open water). `0.6` ⟺ **α≈0.32**, i.e. **suburban / partly-sheltered** ground (courts near buildings, trees, fences) — the common case. **Honest caveat:** a truly **open field** is α≈0.14 → factor ≈**0.79**, so on open ground the app under-reads court wind ~20% and rings a touch too eagerly; a court boxed in by buildings is the reverse. `0.6` is the single defensible middle for v1; per-court _shelter calibration_ is v2.
- **4–6 km/h dropdown (court-level).** The scale is anchored to **BWF's** competition standard: hall **air movement ≤ 0.2 m/s = 0.72 km/h** over the court ([BWF Statutes §5.3.4](https://system.bwfbadminton.com/documents/folder_1_81/Statutes/CHAPTER-5---TECHNICAL-REGULATIONS/Section%205.3.4%20-%20Specs%20for%20Int'l%20Standard%20Facilities.pdf)). **6 km/h ≈ 1.7 m/s** = top of Beaufort "light air", the realistic ceiling for casual outdoor shuttle play, and the **default** (rings most readily — better to ring and let the user judge than to over-skip); **4 km/h ≈ 1.1 m/s** = strictest. **Why the floor is 4, not 1 (2026-07-14):** court wind <4 would need an additive gust floor (its `2.2×` falls under the near-surface gust baseline, below), so it would almost never ring — and that sub-4 range was also the only part never covered by the ring-rate validation. Dropped rather than propped up with a magic constant.
- **Gust guard `2.2 × raw_speed_limit` (no floor).** A calm average with hard gusts still ruins a rally, so gusts get a second gate. Near-surface **gust factors** (peak ÷ mean) over open terrain are typically **~1.4–1.7** ([gust-factor overview](https://www.sciencedirect.com/topics/engineering/gust-factor)); `2.2×` sits _above_ normal gustiness, so it blocks only an _abnormally_ gusty morning. **Why no additive floor:** an earlier `max(…, 12)` existed only because court wind 1–3 have a `2.2×` below the **~12–15 km/h near-surface gust baseline** — gust factors rise as mean wind drops ([AMS](https://journals.ametsoc.org/view/journals/apme/7/3/1520-0450_1968_007_0372_tvogfw_2_0_co_2.xml)), and live Open-Meteo gusts for BLR/Tonk/Jaipur bottomed out at ~14–20 km/h (mornings 23–30). Dropping the sub-4 settings removed the need for that region-flavoured constant, leaving one clean multiple.
- **Whole-km/h decision (2026-07-13).** Speed & gust and their caps are **rounded to whole km/h for both the decision and the display**. The API's 0.1-km/h precision is more than a "calm enough?" gate needs, and rounding both sides before a strict `>` makes the shown numbers agree with the decision _by construction_ (no "skipped at 15 vs ≤15" contradiction). Cost: the skip boundary coarsens to integer resolution, always ≤~0.8 km/h _more lenient_ — harmless since the guard's job is only to sit above the ~11–14 band.
- **All four numbers, always shown (2026-07-13).** Every data-bearing outcome (ring / too-windy / too-gusty) surfaces **speed and gust, each vs its cap** — "wind 3 (≤4) · gusts 16 (≤15) km/h" — in the skip notification, the ring body/screen, and history. A no-data skip shows "could not check the wind". Trust = show the full picture, not just the metric that tripped.
